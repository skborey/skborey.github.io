Hobby

Apart from my professional life, I’m passionate about running.

I started running without any specific purpose—not to get healthier or lose weight—just to run. My journey truly began when I completed my first 10km. It wasn’t fast, and it wasn’t easy—my legs hurt, but I wasn’t overly tired. At that moment, I realized I could do it, and I loved the feeling. It made me want to run again and again. However, after a month, my motivation began to fade. That’s when I understood that to maintain this habit, I needed discipline. But discipline alone wasn’t enough to make running enjoyable, so I approached it creatively by setting my own challenges. One of my first challenges was to run five 10km sessions per week consistently. I stuck with it for three months and felt a sense of accomplishment. Another challenge I set for myself was to reach 100km in a week without any injuries—a goal that pushed my limits while teaching me the importance of balance and recovery. Running has become more than just a habit for me; it’s a way to challenge myself, grow, and stay connected to something I truly enjoy.

570 Academic Word List

This is my hobby project, inspired by my preparation for an English test. During my studies, I discovered the 570 Academic Word List, a collection of words commonly used in academic contexts. To make learning these words more engaging, I created this game. Key Features: A random word is displayed along with three possible meanings. If a word is answered correctly three times, it is marked as "learned" and won't appear again. The game is designed to help learners focus on mastering unfamiliar words effectively.
